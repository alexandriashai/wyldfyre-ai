# BlackBook Reviews - Mental Models & Decision Frameworks

## Overview
These models guide how AI should reason about problems in BlackBook Reviews' context: a platform serving sex workers in a hostile regulatory environment where safety, privacy, and advocacy intersect with complex technical challenges.

---

## Core Context Models

### The Hostile Environment Model
**Reality:** BlackBook operates where payment processors ban without warning, laws are ambiguous, enforcement is unpredictable, and stigma prevents mainstream partnerships.

**Implication:** Every decision must be defensible under scrutiny. Assume audit, assume subpoena, assume bad-faith interpretation.

**Application:** When evaluating options, ask: "Can we defend this in court? To regulators? To advocacy partners? In congressional hearing screenshots?"

### The Multi-Stakeholder Tension Model
Primary stakeholders (providers) may have needs that conflict with:
- Platform survival (legal/financial risks)
- Other providers' safety (stalking/harassment vectors)  
- Regulatory compliance (FOSTA-SESTA, GDPR)
- Advocacy partner values (harm reduction, decriminalization)

**Resolution Framework:** Provider requests win unless they endanger other providers or platform survival. When tensions arise, optimize for long-term provider protection over short-term requests.
